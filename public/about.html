Our Story

Born from the Ocean,
Built by Hand

Peace Surfers began with a single conviction: that the gear you carry into the water should carry meaning. We started as surfers ourselves — people who understood the particular joy of a well-shaped board, a wax bar made with care, a leash that felt like it was made for your wrist alone. What we found in most surf stores was the opposite: rows of identical product, manufactured far from any coastline, designed by committees who had never felt a dawn swell. So we built something different. Peace Surfers is a hobies surf shop rooted in handmade culture — a place where every item on the shelf began as someone's idea of the perfect thing, shaped slowly and with intention.

We believe the best surf store isn't the biggest one. It's the one that knows its craft, respects its community, and stocks products that tell a story. Each piece we carry reflects that wavelength — handmade, honest, and tied to the ocean in a way mass production never could be.

Every board wax, every stitched leash, every shaped fin — each one traces back to a moment of honest craft. This is how Peace Surfers came to be.

All Products
Chapter 01

First Experiments in the Garage

It started with leftover wax, salvaged neoprene scraps, and a kitchen table. Long before Peace Surfers became a surf store, it was a personal obsession — shaping small handmade accessories that actually worked in the water. The first prototypes were rough, honest, and full of salt.

Chapter 02

The Local Surf Community Took Notice

Word spread through the lineup. Local surfers at our home break started asking where to get the gear. That early local support — trading pieces at the carpark, gifting fins to friends — gave Peace Surfers its first real identity as something the surf community genuinely wanted.

Chapter 03

Refining Every Material

Cheap materials don't survive the ocean. We spent seasons testing — natural waxes, sustainably sourced cords, hand-dyed fabrics that hold colour through salt and sun. Refinement wasn't a phase, it became a philosophy: every material earns its place in the product or it doesn't come with us.

Chapter 04

A Distinct Artisanal Surf Identity

What began as garage experiments grew into a real surf store with a clear soul. Peace Surfers today is a wavelength surf shop built on handmade integrity — each product still shaped, stitched, or crafted by hand. Not mass-produced. Not generic. Just honest goods made for people who live for the waves.

Our Beliefs

The Way We Make.
The Way We Live.

01

Made with Integrity

Every product that leaves our hands carries the weight of a choice — to do it right. At Peace Surfers, we work with materials sourced honestly, shaped by hand, and finished with patience. There are no shortcuts in a good board bag or a wax that holds. We believe the surf store should stand behind what it sells, the way the ocean stands behind every wave — completely, without compromise.

02

Stay Close to the Sea

The ocean is not a backdrop — it is our workshop, our compass, and our quality test. We paddle out before we open the doors. We watch the swell before we shape the next piece. Staying close to the sea means our work is always honest: the products we make are tested in real water, by real surfers, in real conditions. Wavelength and tide are part of every design decision we make.

Nothing we create is meant to sit on a shelf and look good. It is meant to get wet, get used, and earn its place in your quiver.

03

Objects of Ritual, Not Disposal

The surf ritual begins before you hit the water. The way you wax your board, the bag you roll your wetsuit into, the cord you thread through your fin — these are acts of care. We make things that deserve that care in return. Our products are not disposable commodities. They are companions in a practice that asks everything of you. Treat them well, and they will carry you further than you expect.

Our Community

Built on Salt, Sun & Shared Mornings

Peace Surfers was never just a surf store — it grew out of a circle of wave-riders who believed that handmade goods, honest craft, and early-morning sessions belong together. We are part of a real ocean-going community, and every product we make carries that spirit forward.

Local Surf Culture, First

We show up before sunrise. We know the breaks, the tides, and the faces. Our roots run deep in the local surf scene — from the hobies surf shop era to today — and that intimacy shapes every board bag, wax comb, and handstitched leash we craft.

Maker Relationships

Every item in our wavelength surf shop is made by hand — by people we know by name. We collaborate with local artisans, shaping partners, and textile makers who share our obsession with quality and the ocean. No mass production, no shortcuts, just genuine craft.

The Ocean Is Our Gathering Place

Whether it's a flat-day paddle, a dawn patrol session, or a post-surf coffee on the sand, Peace Surfers is wherever the community gathers. We belong to the innerlight surf shop spirit — the idea that the best things happen when surfers come together around something real.
